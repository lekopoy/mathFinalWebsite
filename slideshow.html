Computer Bases

Computer Bases are a fundemental part of our society, with their being—

Decimal

Decimal(Base-10) is the traditional number system used by humans, and it is very important to arithmetic. It features numbers 0-9, allowing you to do math such as 1+1=2. This isn't used directly by computers but is used for humans to understand computers.

Hexadecimal

Hexadecimal(Base-16), is a more enriched yet confusing positional numeral system. It features numbers 0-9 same as Base-10 and includes the letters A to F in the alphabet, representing digits 10-15, 0x4A = 74. This is massively more present in computers than Base-10.

Binary

Binary(Base-2) is the most basic numeral system, it uses the digits 0 & 1. A number can be represented in 8-bit binary like this 00111111 = 63. It's also rarely used in anything but computer science.

Invention of the Modern Computer

Konrad Zuse is regarded as the inventor of the modern computer. Now the term "modern computer" does not refer to our current electronics, however it refers to the introduction of binary and boolean logic into computers. Computers had previously been incorperating Base-10 into the machine; leading to much more complicated systems.

Boolean Algebra

Boolean Algebra is the mathematics applied to Base-2 allowing for arithmetic in Base-2. It was introduced by George Boole back in 1847, and has since been expanded upon. We use Boolean Algebra with Transistors to create Logic Gates. Logic gates have multiple types with—AND, OR & NAND being some of the more prominent ones. Inside a modern day computer lies hundreds of millions to billions of Logic Gates.
